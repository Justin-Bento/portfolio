## What Did I Decide To Do?

I figured out I can battle out my CSS skills by using cssbattle.dev. to help me figure out where I am with web development code. Doing this gave me a fun and exciting way to test your current knowledge with CSS. My rules for this project were simple; Have a time-limit of 25min for each challenge. Only look at MDN documentation 3x times during a challenge. Solely rely on documentation. CSSBattle also had some suggestions to help improve your ranking on their website,

1. Omit the last semicolon
2. Omit the previous closing tags
3. Remove double quotes6
4. Shorten selectors with made up attributes
5. position: fixed beats position: absolute, mostly
6. Play with the units
7. You don't always need to add an HTML element to style!
8. Omit the 'px' unit

**Disclaimer:** CSSBattle strategies should not be used in real projects. They primarily help you in CSSBattle code-golfing. In the real world, you'll probably use a minified to make your files smaller.

Once I figured that out, I then started and headed into the battlegrounds

## Should You Use CSSBattle?

CSSBattle is a great tool for people with experience. As you challenge your knowledge. with CSS. Testing yourself may help you get back into web development as it saved me. it helped me to get back into developmet and not dread reading documentation. This challenge also taught me new tips and tricks about CSS that I never knew existed.

However, I would not recommend it to someone starting out. There are weird convections may not be great for learning CSS. I would advocate for you to use freecodecamp. Thank you for reading my blog post and stay safe.
